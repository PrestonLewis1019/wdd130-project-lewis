Type one Diabetes is an autoimmune disease where the body attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas, resulting in little to no insulin production. This causes high blood sugar because glucose cannot enter the body's cells for energy. Genetic factors can increase the risk, and environmental factors like viral infections may act as a trigger.

Symptoms include

  • Extreme thirst
  • Increased hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue or tiredness
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores or wounds
  • Fruity odor on the breath
  • Dry, itchy skin
